Biography

Jaimi Grether freelances in North Carolina on the violin. She has performed in a number of orchestras including the Carolina Philharmonic, Tar River Orchestra, Raleigh Symphony, Long Bay Symphony, Durham Symphony, and Fayetteville Symphony. In 2020 she has participated in Nathan Cole’s Violympics and Renée Paule-Gauthier’s Music Mastery Experience.

Jaimi began playing violin in school orchestra in Ohio when she was nine. At 13 years old, she began studies with Xiao-Guang Zhu until graduation at 17. Other primary teachers include Emma Rubinstein at Miami University in Ohio and Brian Reagin in North Carolina. She has also enjoyed summer studies with Daniel Vega-Albela at Interlochen Arts Camp, Kati Sebestyen at Académie Músicale de Morges, and Gerardo Ribeiro at Meadowmount.

Jaimi earned a Bachelors of Science in Nursing from Kettering College. She resides in North Carolina, USA with her spouse, Michael, and their four children, whom she homeschools. Her latest project is to learn full-stack coding.
